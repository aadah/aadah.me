The Authors

Abdi-Hakin Dirie

Abdi-Hakin - more casually known as Abdi - was born in Mogadishu, Somalia, the nation's capitol. With the outbreak of the ongoing Somali Civil War, Abdi moved to the States as an early immigrant. After nearly a decade of residence, Abdi became a US citizen and now lives in Cambridge, Massachusetts where he is an undergraduate at MIT studying computer science and engineering. He has interests in artificial intelligence, education, and web development, among other things, and loves to read good science fiction when he is not working.

Ahmad Ali Diriye

Ahmad was born in the town of Beledweyne, Somalia. He also currently makes residence in Cambridge, Massachusetts. Before Massachusetts, Ahmad lived in Pennsylvania for quite sometime, and would periodically travel outside the US for random purposes. Now, Ahmad spends most of his time reading news and sitting in on public lectures on MIT's campus. He enjoys debating religion and ethics whenever he can, and has a passion for the cinema, photography, and languages. He also is a fervent cyclist when the season permits.

The Site

The site is a shared blog between Abdi and Ahmad. Both make posts independent of the other's input, except when they are writing a joint post. Topics are variable, from computer science to photography, with various other miscellanea in between. Abdi is more likely to write posts pertaining to code or academia, while Ahmad will tend to write on complementary topics. Fortunately, neither writes substantially more than the other, so there should be a healthy share of posts between the two.

Periodically, a guest post will be put up on the site. Any assertions made in such posts are not necessarily reflective of the site's positions, but guest writers are encouraged to be interesting, and Abdi and Ahmad both agree that contrarian opinions achieve that best. As the writer and politician Ayaan Hirsi Ali once said, With like-minded people one cannot discuss. Indeed, like-mindedness is quite boring. We strive to avoid it.

The site takes on a light or dark theme depending on the local time. The light theme is up from 6:00-18:00. The dark theme is up during the complementary interval. The theme transitions at the interval boundaries. The times are chosen to (very roughly) correspond to sunrise and sunset hours.
