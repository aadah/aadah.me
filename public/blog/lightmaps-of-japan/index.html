Lightmaps of Japan

A photo essay of my time in Japan.

Abdi-Hakin Dirie

This past summer, while interning as a web developer in Japan, I had the amazing opportunity to travel the country, taking photos along the way of the fairly uninteresting, the incredibly captivating, and the sights lying on the spectrum in between. Most of my time was spent in the hectic Tokyo metropolis, but every once in a while I ventured to more rural areas, enjoying some countryside relief from the bustling megacity. I couldn't capture every location I visited, but a selection of what I did capture is presented below.

Shibuya, Tokyo (渋谷区東京都)

Taito, Tokyo (台東区東京都)

Sumida, Tokyo (墨田区東京都)

Chuo, Tokyo (中央区東京都)

Kyoto (京都市)

Nara (奈良市)

Mount Fuji (富士山)

Himeji (姫路市)

The word "lightmap" is just my roundabout way of saying "photograph".
